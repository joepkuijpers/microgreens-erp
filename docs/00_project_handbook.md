## Project Overview

The Microgreens ERP project is an open and continuously evolving initiative focused on developing a transparent, sustainable, and resilient Food Chain.

Although the project originated as an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system for microgreens production, it has grown into a multidisciplinary platform that integrates biology, engineering, software development, automation, artificial intelligence, scientific research, education, and knowledge preservation.

The project seeks to understand and improve every stage of the Food Chain through observation, research, practical experience, and responsible innovation.

Rather than optimising individual components in isolation, the project views the Food Chain as one interconnected system in which plants, people, technology, knowledge, and future generations continuously influence one another.

The ERP platform provides the operational foundation by supporting cultivation, traceability, inventory management, production planning, reporting, environmental monitoring, and decision support.

The Project Foundation provides the philosophical foundation.

The Strategic Foundation translates philosophy into long-term direction.

The technical documentation explains how these principles are implemented in practice.

Together, these elements form one integrated ecosystem that supports continuous learning, responsible stewardship, and long-term development.

The project therefore aims not only to develop better software, but also to contribute to a better understanding of how technology, biology, and humanity can strengthen one another within a resilient Food Chain.

# Part II — The Growth Cycle

## Introduction

Every project is built upon principles.

The Microgreens ERP project is built upon a continuous cycle of growth.

This cycle provides a shared philosophy that connects every aspect of the project, from scientific research and software development to automation, documentation, education, sustainability, and human flourishing.

Rather than viewing these disciplines as separate activities, the project recognises them as interconnected parts of one continuously evolving Food Chain.

The Growth Cycle provides a common language through which decisions, innovations, and future developments can be understood.

It is not a workflow.

It is not a business process.

It is a philosophy of continuous development.

Every observation creates new understanding.

Every new understanding creates opportunities for development.

Every development enables contribution.

Every contribution creates knowledge that can be shared.

Shared knowledge strengthens the foundation upon which future generations continue building.

The cycle therefore has no beginning and no end.

Every generation inherits the work of those before them while accepting the responsibility to strengthen that foundation for those who follow.

## Observe

Every meaningful improvement begins with observation.

Observation is the conscious practice of paying attention to the world with curiosity, patience, and an open mind.

The project recognises that reliable knowledge cannot be created without first understanding reality as it exists.

Observation extends beyond collecting measurements.

It includes listening, watching, questioning, comparing, experiencing, and recognising patterns that may otherwise remain unnoticed.

The quality of future decisions depends upon the quality of present observations.

The project therefore encourages careful observation of biological systems, technological systems, human behaviour, operational processes, and the relationships between them.

Observation is not performed merely to confirm existing beliefs.

Its purpose is to discover new understanding.

Every observation has the potential to reveal new questions, challenge assumptions, and inspire further learning.

Observation therefore forms the foundation upon which every other pillar of the Growth Cycle is built.

## Understand

Observation gains value when it leads to understanding.

Understanding is the process of transforming observations into meaningful knowledge through curiosity, critical thinking, research, experimentation, discussion, and reflection.

The project recognises that understanding extends beyond collecting facts.

It requires exploring relationships, recognising patterns, questioning assumptions, and continuously seeking deeper insight into the biological, technological, environmental, and human systems that shape the Food Chain.

Understanding is never regarded as complete.

Every answer creates new questions, every discovery reveals new possibilities, and every improvement opens opportunities for further learning.

Knowledge should therefore remain open to continuous refinement as new observations, experiences, and perspectives become available.

True understanding also carries responsibility.

Knowledge should be applied with humility, recognising that no individual or generation possesses complete understanding.

The pursuit of knowledge is therefore not a search for certainty, but a continuous commitment to learning, improving, and strengthening the foundation upon which future generations continue to build.

Understanding transforms observation into meaningful insight and prepares the way for responsible development.

## Experience — The Foundation of Growth

Experience transforms observation into understanding, understanding into action, and action into wisdom.

For this reason, experience is not regarded as one of the Seven Pillars of Growth.

Instead, it is recognised as the foundation that gives every pillar meaning.

Knowledge without experience remains incomplete.

Practical experience provides the context through which observations can be understood, assumptions can be challenged, and meaningful improvements can be developed.

The project therefore encourages learning through direct participation, experimentation, craftsmanship, reflection, and responsible practice.

People should be given opportunities to experience processes before they are automated, delegated, or optimised.

First-hand experience develops understanding, preserves practical knowledge, and cultivates respect for the work performed by people, biological systems, and technology alike.

Experience also creates humility.

It reminds us that understanding is continually refined through practice and that every generation has more to discover.

The Growth Cycle therefore does not move directly from understanding to development.

Experience forms the bridge between them, ensuring that development remains grounded in reality rather than assumption.

## Develop

Development is the continuous process of transforming understanding into meaningful improvement.

The project recognises that every observation and every new understanding creates opportunities to refine, expand, and strengthen the Food Chain.

Development is therefore not limited to technology or software.

It includes the growth of people, biological systems, scientific knowledge, engineering solutions, organisational structures, and practical methods.

Responsible development is guided by purpose rather than novelty.

Innovation should not be pursued simply because something new is possible.

Instead, every improvement should strengthen the resilience, sustainability, transparency, and long-term value of the Food Chain.

Development is an iterative process.

Ideas are explored, tested, evaluated, refined, and continuously improved through observation, experience, and collaboration.

Mistakes are regarded as opportunities for learning rather than failures to be avoided.

The project therefore encourages thoughtful experimentation while recognising that lasting progress is achieved through patience, responsibility, and continuous refinement.

Development ultimately prepares knowledge to become meaningful contribution.

## Contribute

Development reaches its fullest value when it contributes to something beyond the individual.

Contribution is the process through which knowledge, experience, abilities, and resources are applied to strengthen the Food Chain, support others, and create lasting value.

The project recognises that every individual can contribute in different ways.

Contribution may take the form of cultivation, research, engineering, documentation, education, craftsmanship, innovation, mentorship, stewardship, or countless other activities that strengthen the project and society.

No contribution is regarded as insignificant.

Small improvements, thoughtful observations, acts of kindness, shared knowledge, and responsible decisions all strengthen the foundation upon which future developments are built.

Contribution should not be measured solely by economic value or productivity.

Its true value is reflected in the positive and lasting impact it creates for people, nature, knowledge, and future generations.

The project therefore encourages every individual to contribute according to their own abilities, interests, experiences, and stage of development.

A resilient Food Chain is built through the combined contributions of many individuals, each strengthening the whole in their own unique way.

Contribution naturally creates knowledge, experience, and understanding that can be shared with others, allowing the Growth Cycle to continue.

## Share

Knowledge reaches its greatest value when it is shared.

The project recognises that every observation, experience, discovery, success, challenge, and lesson learned has the potential to strengthen the understanding of others.

Sharing is therefore regarded as an essential responsibility rather than an optional activity.

The project encourages the open exchange of knowledge, practical experience, research, documentation, mentorship, and constructive discussion.

By sharing understanding, individuals enable others to continue building upon existing knowledge rather than repeatedly starting from the beginning.

Sharing is not limited to information.

It also includes time, experience, encouragement, craftsmanship, curiosity, responsibility, and opportunities that help others grow.

Knowledge should remain accessible, understandable, and capable of continuous improvement.

As understanding expands through new observations and experiences, previously shared knowledge should also evolve.

The purpose of sharing is therefore not merely to distribute information.

Its purpose is to strengthen collective understanding, encourage collaboration, preserve valuable experience, and provide future generations with a stronger foundation than the one inherited.

Through sharing, individual understanding becomes collective wisdom.

## Strengthen

Every contribution carries the responsibility to leave the foundation stronger than it was found.

Strengthening is the continuous process of preserving what is valuable, improving what can be improved, and increasing the resilience of the Food Chain for present and future generations.

The project recognises that lasting progress is rarely achieved through isolated breakthroughs.

Instead, it emerges through countless thoughtful improvements that collectively increase knowledge, understanding, sustainability, reliability, and trust.

Strengthening extends beyond technology.

It includes strengthening people, relationships, communities, documentation, biological systems, research, craftsmanship, education, and responsible stewardship.

Every improvement should reinforce the integrity of the whole rather than optimise one part at the expense of another.

True progress is measured not only by what is created today, but also by how well that creation continues to serve tomorrow.

The project therefore encourages continuous refinement rather than continual replacement.

Whenever practical, existing knowledge, systems, resources, and experience should be preserved, improved, and built upon before new solutions are introduced.

Strengthening also requires reflection.

By regularly evaluating what has been learned, what has been achieved, and what can be improved, the project remains adaptable while preserving the principles upon which it is built.

Strengthening ensures that every cycle of growth leaves a more resilient foundation for those who continue the journey.

## Future Generations

Every generation inherits a foundation built by those who came before.

The project recognises that today's decisions influence opportunities, challenges, knowledge, and resources available to those who will continue the journey in the future.

Responsibility therefore extends beyond the present.

Every improvement should contribute not only to immediate needs but also to the long-term resilience of the Food Chain and the wellbeing of future generations.

Knowledge, experience, craftsmanship, and values should be preserved, refined, and passed forward so that each generation begins with a stronger foundation than the last.

The objective is not to leave future generations with more technology alone.

It is to leave them with greater understanding, healthier ecosystems, stronger communities, better knowledge, meaningful opportunities, and the freedom to continue learning, improving, and contributing according to the challenges of their own time.

Future generations should never be expected to simply preserve the work of those before them.

They should be empowered to question, improve, and responsibly build upon it.

In doing so, every generation becomes both a beneficiary of the past and a steward of the future.

The Growth Cycle therefore has no final destination.

Every strengthened foundation becomes the starting point for new observation, new understanding, and new opportunities to continue the cycle of growth.

The responsibility of one generation becomes the opportunity of the next.

## Document Lifecycle

Every document within the Microgreens ERP project follows a continuous lifecycle.

Documentation is regarded as a living body of knowledge that develops alongside the project itself.

Rather than being written once and forgotten, documents are continuously reviewed, refined, expanded, and strengthened as new understanding emerges.

The lifecycle of a document follows these stages.

```text
💡 Idea
      │
      ▼
👀 Observation
      │
      ▼
🗣 Discussion
      │
      ▼
📜 Underlying Principle
      │
      ▼
❓ Fundamental Question
      │
      ▼
📂 Correct Document
      │
      ▼
✍ Professional Writing
      │
      ▼
🔍 Review
      │
      ▼
📦 Approved
      │
      ▼
📚 Project Foundation
      │
      ▼
🔄 Continuous Improvement
```

Ideas are encouraged.

Not every idea immediately becomes documentation.

Instead, ideas are explored through discussion until the underlying principle and the fundamental question become clear.

Only then is the knowledge incorporated into the appropriate document.

This approach helps maintain consistency, reduces duplication, and ensures that every document contributes to a coherent body of knowledge.

Documentation therefore evolves through understanding rather than accumulation.

Every revision should strengthen the documentation without compromising its clarity, purpose, or long-term value.

## Documentation Standards

To maintain consistency throughout the project, all documentation follows a shared set of standards.

These standards are intended to improve readability, reduce ambiguity, and ensure that the documentation remains maintainable as the project continues to grow.

## Cross-Referencing

The documentation is designed as one interconnected body of knowledge rather than a collection of independent documents.

Each document should focus on its own purpose while referring readers to related documents whenever additional context is required.

Cross-referencing improves navigation, reduces duplication, and helps maintain consistency throughout the project.

Rather than repeating information across multiple documents, related concepts should be explained once and referenced where appropriate.

For example, the Human Flourishing Strategy discusses the development of people, while the Artificial Intelligence Strategy explains how intelligent systems should support rather than replace human capability.

Both documents address different questions but complement one another.

Similarly, the Automation Strategy, Human-Centred Operations Strategy, Research Strategy, and Data Strategy each focus on distinct aspects of the project while remaining closely connected.

Cross-referencing encourages readers to explore these relationships and understand the project as one integrated system rather than as isolated areas of knowledge.

Every document should therefore strengthen the understanding of the documentation as a whole.

### Language

All documentation is written in English using British English spelling and terminology.

This ensures consistency and accessibility for an international audience.

### Format

Documentation is written in Markdown to ensure compatibility with GitHub and other documentation platforms.

Headings, lists, code blocks, tables, and diagrams should follow a consistent structure throughout the repository.

### Purpose

Every document should answer one fundamental question.

Every chapter should develop one core idea.

Every paragraph should strengthen that chapter.

If information answers a different question, it most likely belongs in another document.

### Consistency

Documents should complement one another rather than duplicate information.

Whenever practical, related topics should be connected through references instead of repetition.

### Timelessness

Documentation should be written in a manner that remains valuable over time.

Implementation details, software versions, and rapidly changing technologies should be separated from the underlying principles whenever possible.

### Accuracy

Documentation should reflect the current state of the project.

Significant changes to software, workflows, architecture, or philosophy should be accompanied by corresponding documentation updates.

### Continuous Improvement

Documentation is never regarded as finished.

As understanding grows, documents should be reviewed, refined, and strengthened while preserving their original purpose and clarity.

### Stewardship

The documentation belongs to the project rather than to any individual.

Every contributor shares the responsibility of preserving its quality, accuracy, and long-term value.

## Introduction

Development within the Microgreens ERP project extends beyond writing software.

It is a continuous process of observation, learning, experimentation, implementation, documentation, review, and improvement.

The objective is not simply to produce new functionality.

The objective is to strengthen the Food Chain through responsible, well-understood, and well-documented development.

Every improvement should contribute to the long-term quality, maintainability, resilience, and transparency of the project.

Development therefore follows a structured methodology that emphasises understanding before implementation, careful planning before change, and continuous refinement after completion.

Technology evolves.

Knowledge evolves.

The project should therefore evolve with them while remaining faithful to the principles established within the Project Foundation.

# Part II — The Growth Cycle

## Introduction

Every project is built upon principles.

The Microgreens ERP project is built upon a continuous cycle of growth.

This cycle provides a shared philosophy that connects every aspect of the project, from scientific research and software development to automation, documentation, education, sustainability, and human flourishing.

Rather than viewing these disciplines as separate activities, the project recognises them as interconnected parts of one continuously evolving Food Chain.

The Growth Cycle provides a common language through which decisions, innovations, and future developments can be understood.

It is not a workflow.

It is not a business process.

It is a philosophy of continuous development.

Every observation creates new understanding.

Every new understanding creates opportunities for development.

Every development enables contribution.

Every contribution creates knowledge that can be shared.

Shared knowledge strengthens the foundation upon which future generations continue building.

The cycle therefore has no beginning and no end.

Every generation inherits the work of those before them while accepting the responsibility to strengthen that foundation for those who follow.
