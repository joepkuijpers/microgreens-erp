# Future technologies

## Why this chapter exists

The project should remain open to new technology without allowing novelty to set its direction. Future tools are valuable when they improve biological understanding, safe operation, accessibility or resilience.

## Start with the problem

Before testing a technology, ask:

- Which biological or operational problem does it address?
- What is the current baseline?
- Could a simpler method answer the question?
- What new maintenance, energy or dependency does it create?
- Can people still understand and override it?

## Candidate areas

Possible developments include:

- improved plant-light and environmental sensing;
- computer vision for germination, spacing and crop uniformity;
- portable spectroscopy or laboratory methods;
- low-power rack nodes;
- robotics for repetitive handling;
- energy storage and demand coordination;
- new preservation and packaging methods;
- AI-assisted pattern recognition and planning.

Listing a technology does not mean it is approved or planned.

## Small tests first

A future technology should begin with the smallest useful experiment. Compare it with the existing method and define success, failure and stopping criteria. Preserve negative results so the same weak idea is not repeatedly tested.

## AI and automation

AI may find patterns, compare images and suggest explanations. Its outputs must remain linked to source data, model version, confidence and review status.

Automation may act only within tested boundaries. Missing data, disconnection or uncertainty must lead to a safe state. People remain responsible for important biological and operational decisions.

## Avoid dependency

Prefer open formats, documented interfaces, replaceable components and understandable operation. A useful technology should strengthen knowledge rather than lock the project into one supplier or hidden model.

## Adoption decision

Adopt only when the benefit is demonstrated, risks are acceptable, maintenance is realistic and the result fits the project philosophy. Otherwise retain it as an experiment or stop.
