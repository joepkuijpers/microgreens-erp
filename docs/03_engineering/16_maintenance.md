# Maintenance

## Why maintenance matters

Maintenance keeps observations trustworthy, equipment safe and biological production stable. It is not only repair after failure. Cleaning, calibration, software care and learning from repeated faults all belong here.

## Maintain by consequence

Effort should match the effect of failure:

- **Critical:** failure may threaten safety, food integrity or major crop loss.
- **Important:** failure disrupts production or weakens essential evidence.
- **Routine:** failure is inconvenient but recoverable.
- **Experimental:** equipment may be removed without affecting production.

This ranking helps focus limited time and spare parts.

## Equipment identity

Every maintained item should have a stable identity connected to its location and ERP record. Record model, supplier, installation date, manuals, calibration state, service history and replacement parts.

## Useful maintenance

A task should state:

- what is inspected or serviced;
- why it matters;
- required interval or trigger;
- safe isolation method;
- acceptable result;
- action when it fails.

Avoid copying the same long procedure into many engineering chapters. The detailed task belongs in the maintenance record; other documents should link to it.

## Cleaning and measurement quality

Cleaning is part of maintenance when dirt, moisture or residues can affect hygiene, airflow, light, cooling or sensing. Instruments also need inspection for drift, blocked openings, damaged cables and expired calibration.

## Software maintenance

Software, database backups, access control and device configuration require the same discipline as physical equipment. Changes should follow the project workflow: inspect, analyse, approve, make one small change, test, review, commit, push and deploy.

## Repair, replace or redesign

Repair is preferred when it is safe and durable. Replacement is appropriate when support, accuracy or reliability is no longer adequate. Repeated faults indicate a design problem and should lead to redesign rather than endless repair.

## Return to service

After work, verify guards, connections, settings, communication and expected operation before release. Record what changed and whether measurements remain comparable with earlier data.
