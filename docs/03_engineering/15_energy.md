# Energy

## Why energy needs its own view

Lighting, climate control, water movement, computing and future freeze-drying all depend on energy. Energy decisions affect cost, resilience, heat, maintenance and environmental impact.

The goal is not simply low consumption. The goal is useful biological and production output with the least reasonable burden.

## Follow the energy order

1. avoid unnecessary demand;
2. improve layout and timing;
3. select efficient equipment;
4. coordinate major loads;
5. measure the result;
6. consider renewable supply and storage.

This order prevents expensive generation or batteries from compensating for avoidable waste.

## Start with biology

Energy reduction must not undermine plant health, food safety or working conditions. Daylight can reduce lighting demand, but may add heat or unevenness. Lower fan use may save electricity but harm airflow. Every saving should be checked against crop response.

## Main loads

| Load | Main opportunity |
| --- | --- |
| Lighting | Layout, daylight, schedule, dimming and crop-stage control |
| Climate | Reduce unwanted heat and coordinate ventilation |
| Water | Efficient pumps, short routes and purposeful operation |
| Computing | Appropriate hardware and reliable low-power operation |
| Freeze-drying | Full loads, validated cycles and product value |

## Measure enough to decide

Begin with whole-system use and the largest load groups. Useful indicators may include:

- energy per tray;
- energy per fresh or dry kilogram;
- idle consumption;
- peak demand;
- energy lost through failed batches or waste.

Fine-grained meters are justified only when they support a decision.

## Renewable energy and storage

Solar, other renewable sources and batteries may become valuable, but they have purchase, material, space and replacement costs. Their suitability depends on measured demand, timing and local conditions.

Stored energy should first protect critical control, data and safe shutdown. Supplying every process during an outage may be unnecessary or uneconomic.

## Development path

Establish a reliable baseline, improve obvious waste, compare changes with crop output and add generation only after demand is understood. Record assumptions and dates because energy prices and technology change.
