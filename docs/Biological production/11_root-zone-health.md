# Root Zone Health

## Purpose

The root zone forms the biological and physical foundation of plant health.

Healthy roots are responsible for water uptake, nutrient absorption, gas exchange and interactions with beneficial microorganisms. Problems within the root zone often become visible above ground only after significant damage has already occurred.

This document describes the factors influencing root zone health and their importance within the Microgreens ERP project.

---

## Introduction

Although hidden from view, the root zone is one of the most active biological environments in plant cultivation.

Roots continuously interact with:

- Water
- Oxygen
- Growing media
- Beneficial microorganisms
- Nutrients
- Plant pathogens

Maintaining a healthy root environment is essential for sustainable crop production.

---

## Functions of the Root Zone

Healthy roots perform several essential functions.

### Water Uptake

Roots absorb water required for:

- Photosynthesis
- Nutrient transport
- Cell expansion
- Temperature regulation

Insufficient water reduces plant growth, while excessive water limits oxygen availability.

---

### Nutrient Uptake

Roots absorb dissolved nutrients from the growing medium.

Balanced nutrient availability supports:

- Leaf development
- Stem growth
- Chlorophyll production
- Overall crop quality

---

### Gas Exchange

Roots require oxygen for respiration.

Poor aeration reduces root activity and increases the risk of root diseases.

Healthy root systems therefore require a balance between moisture and air.

---

### Biological Interactions

The root zone supports numerous beneficial organisms.

Examples include:

- Beneficial bacteria
- Beneficial fungi
- Mycorrhizae
- Protozoa
- Nematodes

These organisms contribute to nutrient cycling, disease suppression and biological stability.

---

## Factors Affecting Root Health

Several environmental factors influence root development.

### Moisture

Adequate moisture supports water uptake while preventing drought stress.

Excessive moisture may reduce oxygen availability and increase disease risk.

---

### Oxygen

Healthy roots require continuous oxygen supply.

Compacted or saturated growing media reduce oxygen levels and may lead to root decline.

---

### Temperature

Root temperature influences:

- Germination
- Growth rate
- Water uptake
- Nutrient absorption
- Microbial activity

Stable temperatures generally support healthier root development.

---

### Growing Medium

The growing medium influences:

- Water retention
- Drainage
- Aeration
- Root anchorage
- Microbial habitat

Selecting an appropriate substrate is therefore essential.

---

### Water Quality

Water quality affects long-term root health.

Important parameters include:

- pH
- Electrical conductivity (EC)
- Temperature
- Dissolved oxygen
- Mineral composition

Poor water quality may negatively affect both roots and microorganisms.

---

## Root Zone Problems

Common root-zone problems include:

- Root rot
- Oxygen deficiency
- Waterlogging
- Drought stress
- Salt accumulation
- Poor germination
- Root damage
- Pathogen infection

Many above-ground symptoms originate below the surface.

---

## Monitoring

Root health should be evaluated regularly through observations including:

- Root colour
- Root density
- Root branching
- Root odour
- Root firmness
- Root disease symptoms
- Moisture levels
- Substrate condition

Healthy roots are generally white or light-coloured, firm and well-branched.

---

## Prevention

Preventive measures include:

- Appropriate irrigation
- Good drainage
- Adequate aeration
- Healthy seed
- Clean growing media
- Biological balance
- Stable environmental conditions
- Regular monitoring

Preventing root stress is significantly easier than restoring damaged root systems.

- Growing medium

- Moisture measurements

- Disease observations

- Photographs
- Notes

Combining root-zone observations with sensor data enables long-term analysis of cultivation performance.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Root health should be evaluated throughout every cultivation cycle.

Historical observations help identify:

- Recurring cultivation problems
- Environmental relationships
- Successful cultivation practices
- Effects of biological amendments
- Opportunities for optimisation

Improving the root zone improves the entire cultivation system.
