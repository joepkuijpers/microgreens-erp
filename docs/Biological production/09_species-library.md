# Species Library

## Purpose

The Species Library serves as the central biological reference for all living organisms relevant to the Microgreens ERP project.

It provides a standardized framework for documenting plants, pests, beneficial organisms, microorganisms and other species encountered during cultivation, research and biological monitoring.

Rather than focusing on cultivation methods or management strategies, this document provides reliable biological information that supports identification, research and decision-making throughout the project.

---

## Scope

The Species Library may include:

- Crop species
- Weed species
- Companion plants
- Indicator plants
- Carnivorous plants
- Beneficial insects
- Beneficial mites
- Beneficial nematodes
- Beneficial microorganisms
- Pest insects
- Plant pathogens
- Soil organisms
- Aquatic organisms
- Experimental species

Every organism should be documented using a consistent structure to facilitate comparison and future expansion.

---

## Species Classification

Each species should be assigned to one or more categories.

Examples include:

- Crop
- Beneficial organism
- Pest
- Pathogen
- Carnivorous plant
- Companion plant
- Indicator plant
- Microorganism
- Experimental organism

A species may belong to multiple categories depending on its role within the cultivation system.

## Taxonomic Consistency

Scientific names should be used wherever possible to avoid ambiguity.

Common names vary between countries and languages, whereas scientific names provide an internationally recognised standard.

When appropriate, both the scientific and common names should be recorded.

---

## Continuous Expansion

The Species Library is intended to grow continuously throughout the lifetime of the project.

New species should be added whenever they become relevant through:

- Cultivation
- Research
- Biological monitoring
- Scientific literature
- Experimental trials

The objective is to build a comprehensive biological knowledge base that supports both cultivation and future research.

---

## ERP Integration

The Species Library forms the biological foundation for multiple ERP modules, including:

- Crop management

- Disease observations

Maintaining a consistent species reference improves data quality and enables more advanced analysis across the entire ERP system.
