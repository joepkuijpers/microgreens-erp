# Beneficial Microorganisms

## Purpose

Beneficial microorganisms form the invisible biological foundation of healthy plant growth.

They influence nutrient availability, root development, disease suppression and overall crop resilience. Although often overlooked, these organisms are essential for maintaining stable biological cultivation systems.

This document describes the role of beneficial microorganisms within the Microgreens ERP project and their contribution to sustainable cultivation.

---

## Introduction

Every healthy cultivation system contains billions of microorganisms.

Some decompose organic matter.

Others protect roots from disease.

Some improve nutrient availability.

Others stimulate plant growth through biological interactions.

Rather than viewing microorganisms as contaminants, biological cultivation recognises them as important partners in plant production.

---

## Major Groups

Beneficial microorganisms can be divided into several functional groups.

### Beneficial Bacteria

Beneficial bacteria perform numerous functions including:

- Nutrient cycling
- Nitrogen fixation
- Phosphate solubilisation
- Plant growth stimulation
- Disease suppression

Examples include:

- Bacillus spp.
- Pseudomonas spp.
- Azospirillum spp.
- Rhizobium spp.

---

### Beneficial Fungi

Beneficial fungi contribute to healthy root systems and biological balance.

Examples include:

- Trichoderma spp.
- Mycorrhizal fungi
- Saprophytic fungi

These organisms may improve nutrient uptake while reducing pathogen development.

---

### Yeasts

Certain yeast species compete with plant pathogens and contribute to biological stability.

Although less commonly used in microgreen cultivation, they remain important research organisms.

---

## Biological Functions

Beneficial microorganisms contribute to cultivation through several mechanisms.

### Nutrient Cycling

Microorganisms convert organic compounds into forms that plants can absorb.

Without these biological processes many nutrients remain unavailable.

---

### Root Development

Some microorganisms stimulate root growth through natural biochemical interactions.

Healthier root systems generally improve:

- Water uptake
- Nutrient uptake
- Plant stability
- Stress tolerance

---

### Disease Suppression

Beneficial microorganisms compete directly with plant pathogens.

Disease suppression may occur through:

- Competition for nutrients
- Competition for space
- Production of antimicrobial compounds
- Induction of plant defence responses

---

### Biological Balance

A diverse microbial community increases ecosystem stability.

Greater microbial diversity often reduces opportunities for harmful organisms to dominate.

---

## Environmental Factors

Microbial populations are strongly influenced by:

- Moisture
- Temperature
- Oxygen availability
- pH
- Organic matter
- Growing medium
- Water quality

Healthy microbial communities require suitable environmental conditions.

## Applications in Microgreen Production

Potential applications include:

- Seed treatment
- Growing media inoculation
- Root-zone applications
- Disease prevention
- Compost extracts
- Biological amendments

Selection should always be supported by scientific evidence and practical evaluation.

## Monitoring

Microbial populations cannot usually be observed directly.

Monitoring therefore relies on indirect indicators such as:

- Plant health
- Root condition
- Disease incidence
- Laboratory analysis
- Experimental trials

Future laboratory methods may provide more direct microbial measurements.
