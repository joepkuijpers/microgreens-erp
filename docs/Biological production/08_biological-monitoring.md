# Biological Monitoring

## Purpose

Biological monitoring is the systematic observation and recording of living organisms within the cultivation environment.

The objective is not merely to detect problems, but to understand how the biological system changes over time.

Consistent monitoring provides the information required for informed decision-making, continuous improvement and preventive cultivation.

---

## Introduction

Every cultivation environment is constantly changing.

Plants grow.

Pests reproduce.

Beneficial organisms establish themselves.

Microorganisms develop.

Environmental conditions fluctuate.

Routine biological monitoring transforms these observations into structured knowledge that can be analysed across multiple cultivation cycles.

---

## Monitoring Objectives

Biological monitoring aims to:

- Detect pests as early as possible.
- Detect diseases before significant crop damage occurs.
- Evaluate biological control measures.
- Assess plant health.
- Observe biodiversity.
- Identify environmental influences.
- Support continuous improvement.

Monitoring should always be preventive rather than reactive.

---

## Observation Methods

Different monitoring methods provide different types of information.

### Visual Inspection

Routine inspection remains one of the most valuable monitoring techniques.

Observations may include:

- Plant appearance
- Growth rate
- Leaf colour
- Wilting
- Pest presence
- Disease symptoms
- Root condition

---

### Sticky Traps

Sticky traps provide information about flying insect activity.

They help detect:

- Fungus gnats
- Shore flies
- Whiteflies
- Winged aphids
- Thrips

Trap counts should be recorded consistently over time.

---

### Carnivorous Plants

Carnivorous plants function as living monitoring stations.

Observations may include:

- Species captured
- Capture frequency
- Seasonal variation
- Pest hotspots

Unlike sticky traps, they continue functioning as living organisms while contributing to biodiversity.

---

### Indicator Plants

Indicator plants often respond more rapidly than the primary crop.

Monitoring these plants may reveal:

- Environmental stress
- Nutrient deficiencies
- Pest activity
- Disease development

---

### Beneficial Organisms

Monitoring beneficial organisms helps determine whether biological control has become established.

Observations may include:

- Population development
- Predator-prey relationships
- Distribution
- Reproduction

---

## Monitoring Frequency

Observation frequency depends on cultivation stage.

Suggested routine:

- Daily visual inspection
- Weekly detailed inspection
- Weekly trap assessment
- Continuous sensor monitoring
- Immediate inspection after unusual observations

Monitoring intervals should remain consistent whenever possible.

---

## Recording Observations

Every observation should include sufficient context.

Possible information includes:

- Date
- Time
- Observer
- Cultivation area
- Batch
- Crop
- Observation type
- Species
- Severity
- Photographs
- Notes

Consistent recording improves long-term analysis.

---

## Trend Analysis

Individual observations provide limited information.

Meaningful conclusions emerge by analysing trends across multiple cultivation cycles.

Examples include:

- Increasing pest populations
- Seasonal disease occurrence
- Successful biological control
- Recurring environmental problems
- Long-term improvements

Trend analysis supports evidence-based cultivation decisions.

---

## ERP Integration

Biological monitoring may connect with:

- Grow batches
- Sensor data
- Pest observations
- Disease observations
- Beneficial organism releases
- Harvest records
- Crop quality
- Photographs
- Laboratory results

Combining these datasets allows biological observations to be analysed within their environmental context.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Monitoring is valuable only when observations lead to learning.

Each cultivation cycle should answer questions such as:

- What changed?
- Why did it change?
- Which interventions were successful?
- Which practices should be improved?
- What should be monitored next?

The objective is to improve cultivation through accumulated biological knowledge.
