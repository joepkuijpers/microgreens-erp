# Biological Production

## Purpose

This section documents the biological principles used throughout the Microgreens ERP project.

The objective is to cultivate healthy crops by understanding biological processes rather than relying primarily on corrective interventions.

The documents in this section describe how pests, diseases, beneficial organisms, environmental conditions, biodiversity and cultivation practices interact within the production system.

The goal is continuous observation, measurement and improvement.

---

## Scope

This section includes documentation on:

- Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
- Biological control
- Beneficial organisms
- Carnivorous plants
- Indicator plants
- Companion plants
- Disease monitoring
- Environmental correlations
- Species library
- Biodiversity
- Plant health

---

## Philosophy

Healthy plants are the result of a balanced production environment.

Rather than treating symptoms after problems appear, the project aims to understand the biological system and prevent problems through observation, data collection and continuous improvement.

Every observation contributes to a better understanding of the cultivation process.

---

## Relationship with the ERP

The ERP records biological observations alongside environmental measurements collected from sensors.

Examples include:

- Pest observations
- Disease observations
- Carnivorous plant captures
- Beneficial organism releases
- Temperature
- Relative humidity
- Light intensity
- Air quality
- Corrective actions

This information supports long-term analysis and evidence-based decision making.
