# Decision Records

## Purpose

This document defines how significant engineering, architectural, scientific, operational, and governance decisions are documented throughout the Microgreens ERP project.

Its objective is to preserve the reasoning behind important decisions rather than merely recording their outcomes.

Future contributors should understand not only what changed, but why the change was considered appropriate at the time.

Decision records therefore preserve institutional knowledge and reduce the likelihood of repeatedly solving the same problem.

---

# Philosophy

Every important decision represents knowledge.

Knowledge should not disappear once implementation is complete.

Documenting decisions enables future contributors to understand historical context, evaluate previous assumptions, and build responsibly upon earlier work.

A decision record is therefore considered part of the project's long-term knowledge base.

---

# Objectives

Decision records aim to:

- preserve reasoning;
- improve transparency;
- reduce repeated discussions;
- strengthen governance;
- improve traceability;
- support future reviews;
- record lessons learned;
- preserve historical context.

# Relationship with the Project

Decision Records connect every part of the Microgreens ERP project.

They preserve the reasoning that links philosophy, strategy, architecture, engineering, implementation, governance, and long-term stewardship.

Decision Records should integrate with:

- Project Foundation
- Strategic Foundation
- Documentation Standards
- Project Reference System
- Glossary
- Knowledge Management
- Safety & Resilience
- Stewardship & Governance
- Version Control
- Database Planning
- Revision History

Whenever practical, significant decisions should reference the related project objects through the Project Reference System.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Knowledge evolves.

Technology evolves.

Understanding evolves.

Consequently, decision records should remain reviewable throughout the lifetime of the project.

Reviewing a previous decision does not imply that the original decision was incorrect.

Rather, it acknowledges that new knowledge, experience, research, or technology may justify a better solution.

When a decision changes, both the original reasoning and the updated reasoning should remain preserved.

The project values continuous learning over historical perfection.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Decision Records are intended to become part of the permanent institutional memory of the Microgreens ERP project.

Their purpose extends beyond documenting technical choices.

They preserve the project's collective reasoning, engineering experience, scientific understanding, and governance decisions for future contributors and future generations.

As the project grows, Decision Records should support:

- engineering reviews;
- architectural evolution;
- governance;
- automated traceability;
- knowledge preservation;
- impact analysis;
- future AI-assisted engineering.

The objective is not merely to remember what was decided.

The objective is to preserve why it was decided, how it was validated, what alternatives were considered, and how future contributors can continue strengthening the project with confidence.
