# Research Strategy

## Purpose

Define the role of research within the Microgreens ERP project.

Research exists to improve understanding of biological systems, validate assumptions, and support continuous improvement throughout the Food Chain.

## Vision

Research should become an integral part of daily operations rather than an isolated activity.

Every cultivation cycle provides an opportunity to improve understanding.

Every observation has the potential to contribute to future knowledge.

## Research Philosophy

Research begins with curiosity.

Ideas become hypotheses.

Hypotheses are tested through observation, experimentation, and measurement.

Conclusions should always remain open to revision when new evidence becomes available.

The objective is not to prove assumptions correct.

The objective is to improve understanding.

Research is the continuous refinement of understanding.

The purpose of research is not to eliminate the unknown, but to continuously improve our understanding of it.

Every discovery expands the boundaries of what remains unknown and inspires the next generation of observations, questions, and discoveries.

## Scientific Thinking

Scientific thinking requires:

- Curiosity
- Observation
- Measurement
- Documentation
- Reproducibility
- Critical evaluation
- Continuous improvement

Research should encourage questioning rather than confirmation.

## Every Cultivation Cycle Is Research

Every cultivation cycle generates knowledge.

Even when no changes are introduced, each cycle contributes additional observations that strengthen confidence in previous conclusions.

Unexpected results should be regarded as opportunities to learn rather than failures.

## Experiment Design

Every experiment should define:

- Purpose
- Hypothesis
- Variables
- Measurements
- Expected Outcome
- Evaluation Criteria
- Conclusions

Experiments should remain as simple as possible while producing meaningful results.

## Observation and Measurement

Reliable observations form the foundation of research.

Whenever practical, observations should be supported by objective measurements rather than subjective interpretation.

Sensors, laboratory analysis, photographs, operational records, and human observations all contribute to understanding.

## Reproducibility

Research should be reproducible whenever possible.

Methods, environmental conditions, equipment, and measurements should be documented sufficiently to allow experiments to be repeated.

## Research Documentation

Research should preserve:

- Research Questions
- Experimental Setup
- Environmental Conditions
- Results
- Conclusions
- Lessons Learned
- Future Recommendations

Knowledge should accumulate rather than disappear.

## Validation

Knowledge should develop through repeated observation.

Conclusions supported by multiple independent observations provide greater confidence than isolated results.

Uncertainty should be documented rather than ignored.

## Open Knowledge

Whenever appropriate, research findings should be shared to contribute to broader understanding of sustainable food production.

Knowledge sharing should encourage collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement.

## Ethics in Research

Research should respect:

- Biological Systems
- Scientific Integrity
- Transparency
- Responsible Reporting
- Environmental Sustainability
- Human Wellbeing

Results should never be intentionally manipulated to support predetermined conclusions.

## Relationship with the ERP

The ERP provides the infrastructure that enables research.

It preserves observations, measurements, experiments, traceability, and historical records.

The ERP supports research.

Research continuously improves the ERP.

Together they create a continuously learning system.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term ambition is to create a research platform where every cultivation cycle contributes to a continuously expanding understanding of biological systems, sustainable food production, and responsible innovation.

Research should become part of normal operations rather than a separate activity.

The Food Chain should continuously improve through evidence rather than assumption.
