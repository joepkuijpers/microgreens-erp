# Sensor Strategy

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the philosophy and long-term approach to environmental observation within the Microgreens ERP project.

Sensors are not implemented to collect measurements for their own sake. 

They exist to improve understanding of biological processes, support evidence-based decision-making, and contribute to a transparent and continuously improving food chain.

## Vision

Observation is the foundation of understanding.

Reliable observations enable meaningful measurements.

Meaningful measurements become data.

Data becomes information.

Information becomes knowledge.

Knowledge supports better decisions.

The long-term objective is to create an accurate digital representation of the growing environment that continuously improves the understanding of cultivation processes.

## Observation Before Automation

Automation should never replace understanding.

Every automated decision should be based on measurable observations whenever practical.

Observation creates understanding.

Understanding enables responsible automation.

## Biological Observation

Plants continuously interact with their environment.

The objective of the sensor platform is to observe these interactions objectively rather than relying on assumptions.

Technology should improve biological understanding rather than replace it.

The growing environment should benefit both plants and people.

Environmental observations should contribute to:

- Plant health
- Human health
- Workplace comfort
- Air quality
- Energy efficiency
- Sustainable operation

## Sensor Selection Principles

Sensors should be selected based on more than measurement accuracy.

Selection should also consider:

- Reliability
- Precision
- Repeatability
- Repairability
- Open interfaces
- Modularity
- Long-term availability
- Energy consumption
- Sustainability

## Data Quality

Sensor measurements should be:

- Accurate
- Repeatable
- Time stamped
- Traceable
- Interpretable

Measurements without sufficient context should not influence automated decisions.

## Sensor Integration

Sensor observations should become part of the historical record.

Whenever possible, observations should be linked to:

- Batch
- Crop
- Growing location
- Time
- Environmental conditions

## Sensor Independence

The project should avoid dependence on specific manufacturers.

Hardware will evolve.

The philosophy of observation should remain unchanged.

## Sensor Lifecycle

Sensors should be regarded as long-term assets rather than disposable components.

As technology advances, newer sensors may become the preferred choice for primary monitoring due to improvements in accuracy, reliability, precision, or functionality.

The introduction of newer technology does not automatically reduce the value of existing hardware.

Whenever technically appropriate, previously deployed sensors should be reassigned to secondary cultivation areas, experimental environments, validation measurements, educational purposes, or other locations where meaningful observations can still be obtained.

Hardware should only be retired when it no longer provides reliable or valuable observations.

By extending the operational lifetime of sensors, the project reduces electronic waste, lowers costs, and supports responsible resource management.

## Scalable Sensor Deployment

The sensor network should remain modular, flexible, and scalable.

Primary cultivation areas should always benefit from the most suitable available technology.

As improved sensors become available, existing hardware should be redeployed to other cultivation areas where continuous monitoring remains valuable but absolute measurement accuracy is less critical.

The objective is to maximise the useful lifetime of every sensor while ensuring that critical observations continue to benefit from the most appropriate technology.

## Efficient Use of Sensor Resources

Environmental observation should maximise knowledge while minimising resource consumption.

Not every cultivation area requires permanent instrumentation.

When a growing environment has been sufficiently characterised, sensors may be reassigned to another cultivation area where new observations provide greater scientific or operational value.

Permanent monitoring should be reserved for parameters where continuous observation contributes meaningfully to research, automation, or operational decision-making.

The goal is not to install more sensors.

The goal is to generate greater understanding with the most efficient use of available resources.

## Continuous Observation

Observation over time provides more value than isolated measurements.

Historical observations create context, reveal trends, and support continuous improvement.
