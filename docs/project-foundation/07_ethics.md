# Ethics

## Purpose

This document defines the ethical principles that guide every strategic, technical, operational and organizational decision within the Microgreens ERP project.

Ethics provide the moral framework for responsible decision-making and ensure that innovation, sustainability and growth never compromise integrity, responsibility or long-term purpose.

## Definition

Ethics define how the project's Vision, Mission, Core Values and Guiding Principles are applied in practice.

Every action should contribute to a Food Chain built on honesty, responsibility, transparency and respect for people, plants, animals, nature and future generations.

## Ethical Principles

The ethical principles of the Microgreens ERP project are organized into complementary areas.

Together they define how responsibility, integrity, transparency and respect are applied throughout the entire Food Chain.

Each area supports the Vision, Mission, Core Values and Guiding Principles while providing practical guidance for responsible decision-making.

## Research Ethics

### Principle

Research should pursue knowledge through honesty, objectivity and repeatability.

### Application

- Report observations truthfully.
- Document both successful and unsuccessful experiments.
- Never manipulate results.
- Encourage constructive feedback.
- Repeat experiments whenever practical.
- Allow evidence to replace assumptions whenever new knowledge becomes available.

## Biological Ethics

### Principle

Living organisms should be treated with respect throughout the entire Food Chain.

### Application

- Work with biological principles whenever practical.
- Minimize unnecessary stress on plants.
- Encourage healthy and resilient plant development.
- Work toward certified organic production whenever practical.
- Respect natural biological processes.
- Technology should strengthen biology rather than replace it unnecessarily.

### Reflection

Respect for living organisms begins with attention.

Healthy cultivation is achieved not only through technology, but also through observation, patience, care and a willingness to understand the needs of the plant.

The project encourages a mindset in which plants are treated as living organisms rather than production units.

## Environmental Ethics

### Principle

The environment should be strengthened rather than exploited.

### Application

- Reduce environmental impact whenever practical.
- Conserve natural resources.
- Encourage regeneration rather than merely maintaining existing conditions.
- Apply GreenScope when evaluating environmental decisions.
- Continuously improve environmental performance.

## Open Source Ethics

### Principle

Knowledge grows when it is shared responsibly.

### Application

- Credit contributors.
- Respect licenses.
- Share improvements whenever appropriate.
- Encourage collaboration.
- Preserve transparency throughout development.
- Welcome constructive feedback.

## Artificial Intelligence Ethics

### Principle

Artificial Intelligence should support informed decision-making rather than replace human responsibility.

### Application

- AI assists analysis.
- Humans remain responsible for decisions.
- AI-generated information should be verified whenever practical.
- Maintain transparency regarding AI-assisted work.
- Protect privacy and sensitive information.
- Use AI to strengthen understanding rather than replace critical thinking.

## Employee Ethics

### Principle

Every contributor shares responsibility for quality, safety and continuous improvement.

### Application

- Work responsibly.
- Support colleagues.
- Share knowledge.
- Encourage continuous learning.
- Take ownership of responsibilities.
- Report mistakes openly so they become opportunities for improvement.

## Customer Ethics

### Principle

Customers deserve honest information, transparent products and respectful communication.

### Application

- Communicate truthfully.
- Avoid misleading claims.
- Support complete traceability.
- Protect customer interests.
- Build long-term relationships through trust.

## Supplier Ethics

### Principle

Long-term partnerships create stronger and more sustainable supply chains.

### Application

- Treat suppliers fairly.
- Encourage responsible sourcing.
- Promote transparency.
- Build relationships based on mutual respect.
- Prefer suppliers whose values align with the project's principles whenever practical.

## Food Ethics

### Principle

Food should contribute positively to human health, society and the environment.

### Application

- Preserve nutritional value whenever practical.
- Minimize unnecessary waste.
- Improve food accessibility.
- Support responsible preservation methods.
- Contribute to a healthier, more sustainable and transparent Food Chain.

> *"Reduction in waste increases taste."*

— Microgreens ERP Food Philosophy

## Data Ethics

### Principle

Data should be accurate, transparent, secure and responsibly managed.

### Application

- Maintain accurate records.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Preserve traceability.
- Document significant decisions.
- Use data responsibly and objectively.
- Never manipulate data to influence conclusions.

## Decision Ethics

### Principle

Ethical decisions should preserve the integrity of the project before pursuing convenience or short-term benefit.

### Application

Before making significant decisions, ask:

- Does this align with the Vision?
- Does this support the Mission?
- Does this respect the Core Values?
- Does this follow the Guiding Principles?
- Does this strengthen Biological Integrity?
- Does this preserve transparency?
- Does this benefit the Food Chain?
- Can the reasoning be openly documented?
- Would this decision still be considered ethical in the future?

Whenever ethical uncertainty exists, choose the solution that best preserves integrity, responsibility, transparency and long-term sustainability.

## Engineering Humility

### Principle

No system is ever complete.

Every solution should remain open to improvement through evidence, experience and continuous learning.

### Application

- Accept constructive criticism.
- Welcome new evidence.
- Challenge assumptions.
- Learn from mistakes.
- Replace existing solutions whenever better alternatives are demonstrated.
- View every improvement as another step rather than the final destination.

## Continuous Improvement Cycle

Every improvement should follow a continuous learning cycle.

Measure.

Learn.

Retreat.

Improve.

Repeat.

Retreat represents the deliberate act of stepping back to reflect, reassess assumptions and evaluate alternative approaches before moving forward.

Continuous improvement is not a straight line.

Sustainable progress requires observation, learning, reflection and adaptation throughout the entire process.

## Ethical Reflections

The following reflections summarize the ethical philosophy of the Microgreens ERP project.

> *"Technology should adapt to biology, not biology to technology."*

> *"Reduction in waste increases taste."*

> *"After calculating and testing sustainability, it becomes time for Sustaindoity."*

> *"Measure. Learn. Retreat. Improve. Repeat."*

## Acceptance

The ethical principles described in this document form a permanent part of the Microgreens ERP Project Foundation.

Before contributing to the project, every contributor is encouraged to read, understand and accept these ethical principles.

Acceptance signifies a commitment to uphold the Vision, Mission, Core Values, Guiding Principles and ethical standards of the project.

Constructive discussion and continuous improvement are always encouraged.

However, any proposed changes to these ethical principles should only be made after careful evaluation to preserve the long-term integrity, identity and purpose of the project.

## Ethical Commitment

The Microgreens ERP project is committed to continuous ethical improvement.

Ethics are not viewed as fixed rules but as a continuous process of learning, reflection and responsible decision-making.

Every significant decision should strengthen trust, preserve integrity and contribute to a more transparent, sustainable and responsible Food Chain.
