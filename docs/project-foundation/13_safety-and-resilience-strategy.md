# Safety & Resilience Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines how safety and resilience are incorporated into every aspect of the Microgreens ERP project.

Safety is regarded as a design principle rather than a feature.

Resilience is regarded as the ability of the Food Chain to continue operating, recover from failures, and continuously improve over time.

Together, safety and resilience guide decisions involving people, biology, food, software, hardware, automation, Artificial Intelligence, data, and operations.

The objective is not merely to prevent failures.

The objective is to design systems that minimise risk, respond responsibly to unexpected situations, recover effectively, preserve knowledge, and strengthen the foundation for future generations.

## Vision

The project seeks to develop a Food Chain that is safe by design, resilient by nature, and continuously improving through observation, learning, and responsible stewardship.

Safety protects people, biological systems, equipment, data, and the environment.

Resilience ensures that the project can adapt, recover, and continue operating despite uncertainty, failure, or change.

Together they provide the confidence required to innovate responsibly while preserving trust, transparency, and long-term sustainability.

## Definition of Safety

Safety is the continuous protection of people, biological systems, food, equipment, information, and the environment from unnecessary harm.

Every decision should first be evaluated for safety before considering efficiency, automation, or performance.

## Definition of Resilience

Resilience is the ability of the project to anticipate change, withstand disruption, recover from failure, preserve essential knowledge, and continue strengthening the Food Chain over time.

A resilient system is not one that never fails.

It is one that fails responsibly, recovers effectively, learns continuously, and emerges stronger.

## Core Philosophy

Safety and resilience are shared responsibilities.

They are not confined to one document, one department, one technology, or one individual.

Every strategy, every design decision, every experiment, every line of code, every hardware component, every automation, and every operational procedure should contribute to a safer and more resilient Food Chain.

The project therefore adopts the following principles:

- Safety before efficiency.
- Prevention before correction.
- Understanding before automation.
- Monitoring before assumption.
- Recovery before replacement.
- Transparency before complexity.
- Learning before blame.
- Continuous improvement over perfection.

These principles provide the foundation upon which the remainder of this strategy is built.
