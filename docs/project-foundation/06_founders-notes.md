# Founder's Notes

## Purpose

This document contains the personal reflections, motivations and observations of the founder of the Microgreens ERP project.

Unlike the other Project Foundation documents, these notes are intentionally personal.

They explain why the project exists, what inspires its development and how experience continues to shape its long-term direction.

The Founder's Notes are not rules.

They provide context behind the Vision, Mission, Core Values and Guiding Principles by explaining the thinking that led to their creation.

## Why This Project Exists

The Microgreens ERP project began with curiosity rather than software.

The original objective was never simply to develop an ERP system.

Instead, the project seeks to understand how biology, technology, sustainability and knowledge can strengthen one another to create a more transparent, resilient and responsible Food Chain.

Over time, software became one tool among many.

Research, cultivation, engineering, documentation, automation and continuous learning all became equally important parts of the same journey.

The project therefore continues to evolve through observation, experimentation and practical experience.

## Personal Philosophy

The founder believes that meaningful innovation begins with understanding.

Technology should never become the destination.

It should remain a tool that strengthens biology, improves sustainability and helps people make better decisions.

Progress should be measured not only by efficiency, but also by understanding, responsibility and the positive impact created for future generations.

## Long-Term Ambition

The long-term ambition extends beyond software development.

The project aims to contribute to a Food Chain that is:

- Transparent
- Sustainable
- Regenerative
- Modular
- Scalable
- Knowledge-driven
- Biologically responsible

As knowledge, experience and technology continue to evolve, the project seeks to make nutritious food more accessible while reducing unnecessary waste and resource consumption.

Freeze Drying, automation, responsible cultivation and continuous research are viewed as complementary tools that support this long-term objective.

Ultimately, the project strives to contribute to a world in which technology strengthens the relationship between people, plants, animals and nature rather than replacing it.

## Evolution

The Founder's Notes are intended to evolve throughout the lifetime of the project.

Some reflections may eventually become official Guiding Principles, Core Values or Design Philosophy.

Others may simply remain reminders of why the journey began.

Both are equally valuable.

## Personal Commitment

The founder is committed to remaining curious, open to new evidence and willing to change direction whenever better understanding is achieved.

Every improvement should strengthen the integrity of the project rather than merely increasing its complexity.

Success is measured not only by what is built, but also by what is learned along the way.
