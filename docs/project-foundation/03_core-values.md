# Core Values

## Purpose

This document defines the fundamental values of the Microgreens ERP project.

These values guide every strategic, technical and operational decision.

They remain constant regardless of changes in technology, software, hardware, production methods or future business activities.

## Definition

Core Values are the permanent principles that define the identity of the Microgreens ERP project.

They provide the foundation for decision-making and ensure that growth never compromises the project's purpose, integrity or long-term vision.

The Core Values are organized into two complementary pillars:

- Character
- Action

Together, they define both who the project is and how it operates.

## Character

These values define the identity and philosophy of the project.

### Transparency

**Definition**

Transparency means that information, decisions, processes and results are communicated openly and documented clearly whenever appropriate.

**Application**

Transparency is maintained through clear documentation, traceable ERP records, documented decision-making and open communication with contributors, partners and customers.

### Respect

**Definition**

Respect recognizes the value of people, plants, animals, nature and future generations.

Every decision should protect and strengthen these relationships whenever practical.

**Application**

Respect influences cultivation methods, workplace culture, product quality, environmental decisions and collaboration.

### Stewardship

**Definition**

Stewardship means responsibly managing knowledge, equipment, energy, materials and natural resources while preserving them for future generations.

**Application**

Stewardship guides purchasing decisions, maintenance, infrastructure planning, resource management and long-term investment.

### Harmony

**Definition**

Harmony seeks balance between people, technology and nature.

Technology should strengthen natural systems rather than replace them whenever practical.

**Application**

Harmony influences cultivation, automation, environmental control, energy use and production system design.

### Interconnectedness

**Definition**

Every part of the Food Chain influences the whole.

Successful solutions recognize the relationships between cultivation, preservation, transportation, knowledge, technology, society and the environment.

**Application**

Interconnectedness encourages systems thinking, cross-disciplinary collaboration and holistic decision-making.

### Long-Term Thinking

**Definition**

Decisions should create lasting value rather than short-term advantage.

Long-term sustainability always takes priority over temporary gains.

**Application**

Long-Term Thinking guides investments, infrastructure, documentation, research and strategic planning.

### Biological Integrity

**Definition**

The project is committed to cultivation based on biological principles while working toward certified organic production whenever practical and achievable.

Natural biological processes should be understood, respected and strengthened rather than replaced unnecessarily.

**Application**

Biological Integrity guides decisions related to cultivation methods, seed selection, growing media, irrigation, environmental control, preservation methods and future certification.

Whenever practical, solutions should preserve the natural integrity of the Food Chain while supporting sustainable and responsible production.

## Action

These values define how the project operates and continuously improves.

### Sustainability

**Definition**

Environmental, social and economic sustainability should remain in balance throughout the entire Food Chain.

**Application**

Sustainability influences energy management, cultivation, preservation, transportation, packaging, infrastructure planning and resource efficiency.

### Standardization

**Definition**

Systems, processes and documentation should remain as uniform as practical.

Standardization improves quality, reliability, maintainability, scalability and data consistency.

**Application**

Standardization applies to production racks, ERP workflows, documentation, hardware configurations, operating procedures and future expansion.

### Quality

**Definition**

Quality is designed into every process rather than inspected afterward.

Consistency and reliability are essential for long-term success.

**Application**

Quality guides software development, cultivation, documentation, hardware integration, testing and operational procedures.

### Continuous Improvement

**Definition**

Every process can be improved.

Learning, evaluation, research and practical experience should continuously strengthen the project.

**Application**

Continuous Improvement supports regular review of software, documentation, production systems, hardware, operational workflows and organizational processes.

### Innovation

**Definition**

Innovation should solve meaningful problems and create lasting value.

Technology is adopted with purpose rather than for novelty.

**Application**

Innovation supports ERP development, automation, research, Freeze Dryer development, hardware integration and future expansion.

### Knowledge Sharing

**Definition**

Knowledge grows when it is shared.

Research, experience and documentation should encourage learning and collaboration whenever appropriate.

**Application**

Knowledge Sharing supports documentation, education, research, collaboration and Continuous Improvement throughout the project.

### Empowerment

**Definition**

People should be empowered to contribute, learn and improve.

Well-designed systems, clear information and accessible knowledge enable better decisions and encourage personal responsibility.

**Application**

Empowerment supports education, documentation, teamwork, innovation, personal development and independent thinking.

## Decision Questions

Before making any significant strategic, technical or operational decision, the following questions should be considered.

### Identity

- Does this decision align with the Vision?
- Does it support the Mission?
- Does it preserve the Core Values?

### Biological Integrity

- Does it strengthen biological principles?
- Does it move the project toward certified organic production whenever practical?
- Does it work with nature rather than against it?

### Long-Term Impact

- Will this still be the right decision in five or ten years?
- Does it improve long-term sustainability?
- Does it preserve future flexibility?

### Operational Excellence

- Does it improve quality?
- Does it simplify the system?
- Can it be standardized?
- Is it modular?
- Is it scalable?

### Transparency

- Can the decision be documented?
- Can the reasoning be explained?
- Can future contributors understand why it was made?

### Resources

- Does it reduce unnecessary waste?
- Does it improve energy efficiency?
- Does it use resources responsibly?

### Knowledge

- What can be learned from this decision?
- Can the outcome improve future decisions?
- Should the results be documented for future reference?

Every significant decision should be evaluated consciously rather than made by habit or convenience.

## Decision Framework

The Core Values are intended to work together rather than independently.

Every significant decision should be evaluated against all Core Values whenever practical.

When values appear to conflict, decisions should follow the framework below.

### Step 1 — Preserve Identity

Determine whether the decision protects the fundamental identity of the project.

Evaluate:

- Biological Integrity
- Respect
- Stewardship
- Transparency

If the decision compromises these values without a compelling justification, it should be reconsidered.

### Step 2 — Preserve the Future

Evaluate the long-term consequences.

Consider:

- Long-Term Thinking
- Sustainability
- Harmony
- Interconnectedness

Prefer solutions that strengthen the Food Chain over time rather than providing only short-term benefits.

### Step 3 — Improve Operations

Evaluate operational excellence.

Consider:

- Standardization
- Quality
- Continuous Improvement

The preferred solution should improve consistency, maintainability, reliability and long-term efficiency.

### Step 4 — Enable Growth

Evaluate opportunities for responsible growth.

Consider:

- Innovation
- Knowledge Sharing
- Empowerment

Growth should strengthen the project without compromising the previous Core Values.

## Conflict Resolution

The preferred solution is one that preserves the greatest balance between all Core Values rather than maximizing a single value.

Whenever Core Values appear to conflict, priority should generally be given in the following order:

1. Preserve the project's identity.
2. Protect the long-term future.
3. Improve operational excellence.
4. Enable sustainable growth.

Every significant decision should be documented together with the Core Values that influenced the outcome.

The Decision Framework is intended to support thoughtful and consistent decision-making rather than replace professional judgment.

Every significant decision should strengthen the long-term vision of the project while preserving its identity, integrity and purpose.
