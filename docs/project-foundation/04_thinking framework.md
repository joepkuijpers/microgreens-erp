# Thinking Framework

## Purpose

This document defines the thinking methodology of the Microgreens ERP project.

The Thinking Framework provides a structured approach for understanding problems, evaluating evidence and making informed decisions.

Rather than prescribing solutions, it encourages disciplined thinking, continuous learning and responsible innovation.

## Definition

Thinking precedes decision-making.

Every strategic, technical and operational decision should begin with understanding before action.

The following principles summarize the project's preferred way of thinking.

## Thinking Principles

### Scope Before Dive

Understand the complete system before focusing on individual components.

Always begin with the broader context before solving specific problems.

---

### Know Before Believe

Verify assumptions through observation, evidence, testing and research before accepting them as truth.

Knowledge should guide decisions rather than assumptions.

---

### Root Before Fruit

Strong foundations produce sustainable results.

Address underlying causes before expecting lasting outcomes.

---

### Gain Feedback Before Overdrive

Seek meaningful feedback before amplifying ideas, assumptions or decisions.

Feedback improves understanding.

Overdrive amplifies misunderstanding.

Encourage experimentation, discussion and open collaboration before expanding systems or committing significant resources.

---

### Mediate Before Mitigate

Seek understanding and balance before applying corrective actions.

Resolve causes rather than only reducing symptoms.

---

### Observe Before Automate

Understand a process completely before automating it.

Automation should improve understanding rather than replace it.

---

### Measure Before Optimize

Measure first.

Optimize second.

Measure again.

Never optimize assumptions.

---

### Learn Before Scale

Growth should follow validated learning rather than repetition.

Observe.

Measure.

Learn.

Improve.

Expand.

---

### Origin Before Optimization

Understand why a system exists before attempting to improve it.

Optimization should preserve purpose while improving efficiency.

## Continuous Thinking Cycle

The preferred thinking cycle of the project is:

Scope.

Observe.

Measure.

Learn.

Retreat.

Improve.

Repeat.

Retreat represents the deliberate act of stepping back to reflect, reassess assumptions and evaluate alternative approaches before continuing.

Continuous improvement is therefore viewed as an iterative learning process rather than a straight line.

## Philosophy

The objective of the Thinking Framework is not to provide predetermined answers.

Its purpose is to improve the quality of questions that guide every decision.

Better questions produce better understanding.

Better understanding produces better decisions.
